## Weight Budget Failure

PROPULSION recommends Kawasaki I4 turbo engines at 290-310 lbs installed weight per engine.

**Current budget:** 120 lbs total (both engines)  
**Proposed weight:** 580-620 lbs total (both engines)  
**Increase:** +460-500 lbs

## Impact Analysis

| Parameter | Current | With Kawasaki | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| Generator engines | 120 lbs | 580-620 lbs | +460-500 lbs |
| Empty weight | 1,230 lbs | 1,690-1,730 lbs | +460-500 lbs |
| Useful load | 1,200 lbs | 470-500 lbs | -700-730 lbs |

**Mission failure.** Cannot carry 4 people and fuel with 470 lbs useful load.

## Power vs Weight Analysis Required

PROPULSION claims Honda 400cc "power inadequacy" for single-generator-failed operation.

**Show me the calculation:**
1. What is the actual continuous power requirement in kW?
2. What is single-generator-failed power available with Honda vs required?
3. What flight envelope restrictions result from Honda power limits?
4. Is 460-500 lbs weight penalty justified for that capability?

## Alternative Solutions

Before accepting 500 lb weight penalty:

**Option 1:** Three Honda engines instead of two Kawasaki
- 3 × Honda ≈ 180 lbs vs 2 × Kawasaki ≈ 600 lbs
- Triple redundancy vs twin
- Single-engine-failed has two remaining generators

**Option 2:** Accept power-limited envelope with Honda
- Normal ops: full capability
- Single-generator-failed: reduced power cruise, VFR only
- 500 lbs weight savings enables 4-person missions

**Option 3:** Mission change to 2-seat configuration
- Accept Kawasaki weight
- Reduce cabin, interior, seats to recover useful load
- Different airplane than originally specified

## STRUCTURES Position

**Will not approve** Kawasaki engines without:
1. Specific power requirement calculation showing Honda inadequacy
2. System-level offset of 450 lbs minimum from other systems
3. Bill's explicit approval of useful load reduction to 470 lbs

Weight is zero-sum. Show me what gets removed to pay for this.
