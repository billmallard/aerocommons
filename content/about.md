## About AeroCommons

AeroCommons is a nonprofit organization dedicated to advancing open-source experimental aviation through education, open publication, and accessible design tooling. AeroCommons provides communications infrastructure, publishing services, and shared technical resources — including AI-assisted design tools — to independent builders and designers working in their private capacity. All design information published through AeroCommons is offered for educational purposes. Individual builders retain full responsibility for their own projects and aircraft.

**Status:** Non-profit formation in progress (501(c)(3) pending).  
**Founded:** 2026, Keller, Texas.  
**Contact:** [contact@aerocommons.org](mailto:contact@aerocommons.org)

### What we believe

- Aviation engineering knowledge should be freely available to anyone who wants to learn and build.
- AI driven processes radically lower the engineering and development costs to bring new ideas to life, making truly new innovation financially feasible.
- AI driven processes also radically accelerate the time table between idea and physically realizing the idea in ways we cannot yet imagine.
- The FAA Experimental Amateur-Built program is one of the most important 
  innovation frameworks in all of engineering.
- Open-source methodology — published design files, decision records, 
  and analysis outputs — makes better aircraft, not just cheaper ones.
- Properly applied software development methodologies work well with iterative aircraft development.
- Every homebuilder who publishes their work makes the next builder's 
  job easier.
