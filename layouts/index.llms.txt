## About

AeroCommons is an open-source experimental aircraft project. The MAOS (Modular Aerospace Operating System) is a four-seat, series-hybrid, IFR-capable aircraft designed for homebuilders. Development is coordinated by an AI-assisted design board using OpenClaw.

## Design Board Agents

The MAOS design board consists of seven AI agents, each responsible for a domain:

- CHAIRMAN: Board orchestrator — runs formal review meetings, coordinates cross-domain decisions
- AERO: Aerodynamics — airfoil selection, drag analysis, lift calculations
- STRUCTURES: Structural engineering — weight budget, material selection, load paths
- PROPULSION: Propulsion systems — engine candidates, hybrid architecture, thermal management
- SYSTEMS: Electrical and avionics — bus voltage, power distribution, flight systems
- MANUFACTURING: Production engineering — tooling, processes, homebuilder feasibility
- SAFETY: Safety and certification — failure analysis, risk assessment, advisory compliance
